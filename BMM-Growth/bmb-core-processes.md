## 1. Sales Process

*How a prospect becomes a signed, paying client.*

1. **Discovery Call/Zoom** — First conversation with the prospect. Understand who they are, what they're doing, and why they reached out.

2. **Identify Client Type** — Categorize the client so you're quoting and pitching the right thing:
   - Live Streaming (single event or recurring)
   - Screen Projection
   - PPV
   - Ongoing/Retainer (company with recurring needs)

3. **Identify Specific Needs** — Nail down the exact production(s), event(s), or product(s) they need first. Don't propose until this is clear.

4. **Propose, Confirm & Close** — Present the solution, answer questions, get verbal agreement on scope and price before anything is written up.

5. **Create Proposal or Contract + Invoice** — Formalize the agreement. Send both the contract and invoice together.

6. **Confirm Contract Signed & Payment Made** — Nothing moves forward until both are confirmed. This is the hard gate.

7. **Hand Off to Delivery** — Trigger the Production/Delivery Process. Client is now active.

*Note: Step 6 is the most critical gate — no work begins without a signed contract and confirmed payment. Step 7 is the bridge into the Production/Delivery Process.*

## 2. Production/Delivery Process

*How a confirmed, paid project gets executed and closed out.*

1. **Scope Review & Resource Confirmation** — Review the full project scope. Confirm you have everything needed. List any gaps — equipment, crew, software, access. Identify anything outside scope early and have those conversations before work begins. Don't let surprises surface on event day.

2. **Gameplan & Schedule** — Build the production plan. Assign roles, set internal deadlines, lock the run-of-show timeline. Everyone involved knows what they're doing and when.

3. **Execute** — Deliver within the plan. Follow the schedule, hit the milestones, run the production.

4. **Post-Production Finalization** — Complete any post-event deliverables — recordings, edits, VODs, platform uploads, client-facing assets.

5. **Financial Closeout** — Pay out team members, third parties, and cover any software/hardware fees tied to the project.

6. **Budget Debrief** — Build the final budget showing all revenue in and all costs out. Use it to assess whether pricing was right and flag anything to adjust on the next similar project.

*Note: Step 1 is the most critical gate — catching scope gaps and out-of-scope items early protects the project from bleeding money. Step 6 feeds directly into smarter pricing decisions over time.*
