## The Full EOS Toolbox

EOS provides a specific set of practical tools — no buzzwords, no theory. Each tool is tied to one of the Six Components and serves a specific purpose in the operating system.

---

## Vision Tools

### V/TO — Vision/Traction Organizer
**Component:** Vision  
**Format:** 2-page document  
**Frequency:** Updated quarterly and annually

The master strategic document for the entire business. Contains answers to the Eight Questions:
1. Core Values
2. Core Focus (Purpose + Niche)
3. 10-Year Target
4. Marketing Strategy (target market + 3 uniques + proven process + guarantee)
5. 3-Year Picture
6. 1-Year Plan
7. Quarterly Rocks
8. Issues List

**How to use it:**
- Complete with the full leadership team during a facilitated session
- Share the entire V/TO with all employees — no secrets
- Review the top half (long-term vision) annually; review the bottom half (execution) quarterly
- Post it in the office and reference it in every planning conversation

## People Tools

### Accountability Chart
**Component:** People  
**Format:** Hierarchical diagram of seats and accountabilities  
**Frequency:** Updated as structure evolves

Replaces the traditional organizational chart. Instead of names and titles, it maps:
- **Functions** the business must perform to succeed
- **Seats** (roles) that own those functions
- **3–7 accountabilities** per seat (outcomes, not tasks)
- Who currently fills each seat (a person's name goes in the seat — not the other way around)

**How to use it:**
1. Start blank — identify the major functions of the business
2. Group related functions under seats
3. Define 3–7 accountabilities for each seat
4. Place names — some people may fill multiple seats temporarily
5. Identify gaps (empty seats) and doubles (one person in too many seats)
6. Use it to drive hiring decisions and structure conversations

**Key insight:** The Accountability Chart shows you where structure is missing before problems appear.

### People Analyzer
**Component:** People  
**Format:** Spreadsheet/grid  
**Frequency:** Used quarterly or when making people decisions

A structured tool for evaluating whether someone is the **Right Person** (core values fit) in the **Right Seat** (GWC fit).

**How it works:**
1. List your Core Values across the top (3–5 values)
2. List team members down the left side
3. Grade each person against each Core Value: **+**, **+/-**, or **-**
4. Apply GWC (Get it / Want it / Capacity) to evaluate seat fit

**Grading key:**
- **+** = consistently demonstrates this value
- **+/-** = sometimes demonstrates, sometimes doesn't
- **-** = does not demonstrate this value

**Thresholds:**
- A person with 2+ minuses on Core Values is likely a culture fit issue
- A person who fails any leg of GWC is in the wrong seat

### GWC Assessment
**Component:** People  
**Format:** 3-question evaluation  
**Frequency:** Applied during hiring, performance reviews, and role changes

Three questions, all must be "yes" for a person to belong in a seat:

| Question | What you're looking for |
|---|---|
| **Get It** | Do they intuitively understand the role — how decisions get made, how the team works, what's expected — without needing constant explanation? |
| **Want It** | Do they genuinely desire to be in this role? Is it fulfilling, not just tolerable? |
| **Capacity to Do It** | Do they have the mental, physical, emotional, and skills-based ability to execute this role at the required level? |

**How to use it:**
- Apply during every hiring decision (screen for GWC before skills)
- Use quarterly when reviewing the People Analyzer
- Use before promoting someone — they may GWC the current seat but not the new one

### Delegate and Elevate
**Component:** People  
**Format:** 2x2 matrix  
**Frequency:** Used when a leader is overwhelmed or unclear on their role

A tool for leaders to identify activities to hand off so they can operate at their highest value.

**The matrix:**
| Zone | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Love it / Great at it** | Stay here — this is your highest contribution |
| **Like it / Good at it** | Do it, but look for delegation opportunities over time |
| **Don't like it / Good at it** | Delegate — this drains you even if you perform well |
| **Don't like it / Not good at it** | Delegate immediately — this is urgent |

---

### Crystallizer Assessment
**Component:** People / Leadership  
**Format:** 40-question self-assessment  
**Frequency:** Once, when determining leadership roles

Identifies whether a leader is primarily a **Visionary** or an **Integrator** — the two distinct leadership archetypes in EOS. Used before building the leadership team structure.

## Data Tools

### Scorecard
**Component:** Data  
**Format:** Weekly tracking grid  
**Frequency:** Updated and reviewed weekly in Level 10 Meeting

Tracks 5–15 weekly **leading indicator** metrics that reveal whether the business is healthy before problems compound.

**How to build a Scorecard:**
1. Ask: "What 5–10 numbers, if we hit them every week, would tell us the business is healthy?"
2. Focus on activity-based metrics, not results (results lag behind activity)
3. Assign one **owner** per metric
4. Set a **goal** (target number) for each metric
5. Mark each metric weekly as **on-track** (green) or **off-track** (red)
6. Any metric that is consistently red drops to the **Issues List** for IDS

**Example metrics by function:**
- Sales: calls made, proposals sent, demos completed
- Operations: jobs delivered on time, quality score, tickets resolved
- Finance: receivables over 30 days, cash on hand, burn rate
- HR: open positions, turnover rate, new hire 30-day check-ins

## Issues Tools

### Issues List
**Component:** Issues  
**Format:** Running prioritized list  
**Frequency:** Maintained continuously; reviewed and prioritized weekly

The central repository for every problem, opportunity, and decision that needs attention. Lives at multiple levels:
- **Company-level Issues List** (reviewed in Level 10 Meetings)
- **Departmental Issues Lists** (reviewed in department meetings)
- **Individual Issues Lists** (brought to 1-on-1s)

**Rules:**
- Capture everything — no issue is too small or too large
- Don't solve issues as they arise; put them on the list first
- Prioritize top 3 issues each week for IDS

---

### IDS (Identify, Discuss, Solve)
**Component:** Issues  
**Format:** 3-step conversational process  
**Frequency:** Used in every Level 10 Meeting and whenever issues arise

**Step 1 — Identify (most time spent here)**
- State the root issue — not the symptom
- Use "5 Whys" technique: keep asking "Why?" until the real cause emerges
- Example: "Sales are down" is a symptom. "We have no follow-up process after demos" is an issue.

**Step 2 — Discuss**
- Open conversation — all voices heard
- Stick to facts and relevant perspectives
- No tangents, no rehashing, no political maneuvering
- Stop when everyone understands the issue fully

**Step 3 — Solve**
- Agree on one specific action
- Assign to one owner
- Set a deadline
- Create a To-Do that is tracked to completion
- The issue is permanently resolved — it does not return to the list

**Common IDS failures:**
- Jumping to solve before identifying the root cause
- Discussion turning into argument or defensiveness
- "Solving" without a clear owner or deadline
- The same issue appearing repeatedly (it was never truly solved)

## Process Tools

### 3-Step Process Documenter
**Component:** Process  
**Format:** Simple template  
**Frequency:** Used once per core process, updated as needed

1. **Name the process** clearly (e.g., "New Client Onboarding")
2. **List 5–10 major steps** in order (high-level, not micro-steps)
3. **Assign an owner** — one person responsible for maintaining it

After documenting, conduct a **"Followed by All"** check: walk through each step with all relevant team members to confirm they understand and agree to follow it.

## Traction Tools

### Rocks Template
**Component:** Traction  
**Format:** List of 3–7 quarterly priorities per person  
**Frequency:** Set every 90 days at Quarterly Planning Sessions

**What makes a good Rock:**
- Specific and measurable — you will know definitively if it's done
- Achievable in 90 days with focused effort
- Significant — it moves the business forward meaningfully
- One owner — never assign a Rock to a group

**Rock review in Level 10 Meeting:**
- Simply declare: **On Track** or **Off Track**
- No status updates or explanations in the weekly meeting
- If Off Track for 2+ weeks → drop to Issues List for IDS

---

### Level 10 Meeting Agenda
**Component:** Traction  
**Format:** Fixed 90-minute agenda  
**Frequency:** Weekly (same day, same time, same place)

The Level 10 Meeting is the weekly heartbeat of the EOS system. The name comes from the goal of every meeting being a 10 out of 10 in productivity.

**Fixed Agenda:**
| Section | Time | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Segue / Good News | 5 min | Personal and professional wins — builds connection |
| Scorecard Review | 5 min | Review all metrics — mark on/off track |
| Rock Review | 5 min | Each Rock: On Track or Off Track only |
| Customer / Employee Headlines | 5 min | One good, one bad from customer and team interactions |
| To-Do List Review | 5 min | Confirm all prior To-Dos are done (not updates — done/not done) |
| IDS (Issues, Decisions, Solutions) | 60 min | Solve the top 3–5 issues from the Issues List |
| Conclude | 5 min | Review new To-Dos, rate the meeting 1–10 |

**Rules:**
- Same day, same time, every week — never cancel
- Leadership team attendance is mandatory
- Strictly follow the agenda — no exceptions
- Issues get put on the list during the meeting, not solved ad hoc in early sections

### Organizational Checkup
**Component:** All Six Components  
**Format:** 20-question assessment  
**Frequency:** Quarterly; used to benchmark organizational health

A quick diagnostic that reveals where each of the Six Components is weak. Score 1–5 per question. Helps prioritize which component to focus on next.

### Culture Checkup
**Component:** People / Vision  
**Format:** Culture Quadrant assessment  
**Frequency:** As needed, especially during growth phases

Evaluates company culture health using a quadrant model. Helps identify whether the culture matches the stated Core Values.

### Clarity Break
**Component:** Leadership / Vision  
**Format:** Unstructured solo thinking time  
**Frequency:** Monthly minimum (recommended weekly for leaders)

Not a meeting — it's protected, scheduled time for a leader to **think** rather than react. Step away from the business physically and mentally to work **on** the business instead of **in** it.

**How to use it:**
- Block 2–4 hours in a different environment (coffee shop, park, home office)
- No devices, no agenda — just a notepad
- Questions to ask yourself: "What's working? What isn't? What's the one thing I should be doing more/less of?"
- Capture insights and bring them back to the Issues List or V/TO
