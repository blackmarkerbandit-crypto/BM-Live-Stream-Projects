# EOS — Entrepreneurial Operating System: Overview

**Source:** https://www.eosworldwide.com/

**Purpose:** General reference for implementing EOS across any business

## What Is EOS?

The **Entrepreneurial Operating System (EOS)** is a complete, practical business framework created by **Gino Wickman** and detailed in his book *Traction*. It gives leadership teams a set of simple tools and disciplines to:

- Get everyone aligned on where the company is going
- Build discipline and accountability in executing the plan
- Develop a healthy, functional leadership team

Over **280,000+ businesses** currently run on EOS. More than **3 million copies** of *Traction* have been sold. There are **850+ certified EOS Implementers** worldwide.

---

## The Five Frustrations EOS Solves

EOS was built to address the five most common entrepreneurial frustrations:

1. **Loss of control** — the business running you instead of you running it
2. **People issues** — the wrong people in the wrong seats
3. **Weak profit** — revenue growth without corresponding profitability
4. **Stalled growth** — hitting a ceiling and not knowing why
5. **Broken systems** — inconsistency, repeated mistakes, no scalability

---

## The Three Core Outcomes

Every EOS implementation works toward three fundamental results:

| Outcome | Description |
|---|---|
| **Vision** | Everyone is 100% aligned on where the company is going and how it will get there |
| **Traction** | Every person in the organization has the discipline and accountability to execute on that vision |
| **Healthy** | Your leadership team is cohesive, functional, and trust-based |

---

## The Six Key Components

EOS strengthens six interconnected areas of every business. These are often visualized as the **EOS Model**:

| Component | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Vision** | Strategy, direction, and long-term goals |
| **People** | Culture fit, role clarity, and accountability |
| **Data** | Objective metrics that measure business health |
| **Issues** | Systematic identification and resolution of problems |
| **Process** | Documented, consistently-followed core workflows |
| **Traction** | 90-day execution rhythm and meeting structure |

All six components must be strong for the business to thrive. Weakness in any one area creates drag on the others.

## Who EOS Is For

EOS works best for:

- **Company size:** 10–250 employees (entrepreneurial stage)
- **Ownership:** Privately held, growth-focused companies
- **Mindset:** Leadership teams willing to be honest, change behavior, and embrace structure
- **Industries:** All — EOS is industry-agnostic

## Two Paths to Implement

### Option 1: EOS Implementer (Guided)
A certified EOS Implementer facilitates the process for your leadership team over approximately 2 years. This includes quarterly full-day sessions plus a 2-day annual planning meeting.
- **Cost:** $18,000–$50,000+ per year
- **Best for:** Teams that want faster results and expert facilitation

### Option 2: Self-Implementation
Leadership uses the books, free tools, and EOS Academy resources to run the process themselves.
- **Cost:** Low (books + optional software)
- **Best for:** Budget-conscious teams with high discipline
- **Risk:** Slower progress, easier to skip steps

## Quick Reference: EOS Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Rocks** | The 3–7 most important 90-day priorities |
| **V/TO** | Vision/Traction Organizer — the 2-page strategic plan |
| **Level 10 Meeting** | Weekly 90-minute structured leadership meeting |
| **Accountability Chart** | Roles and responsibilities map (replaces org chart) |
| **Scorecard** | 5–15 weekly metrics tracking business health |
| **IDS** | Identify, Discuss, Solve — the EOS problem-solving method |
| **People Analyzer** | Tool to assess cultural fit and role fit |
| **GWC** | Get it, Want it, Capacity to do it — role readiness test |
| **Visionary** | Big-picture, ideas-focused leader (often the founder) |
| **Integrator** | Execution-focused leader who makes the vision happen |
| **Meeting Pulse** | The structured cadence of weekly, quarterly, and annual meetings |
| **Issues List** | Running list of problems, opportunities, and decisions to solve |
| **Core Processes** | The 5–7 documented workflows that run the business |
