# EOS — Meeting Cadence: The Meeting Pulse

**Source:** eosworldwide.com, ninety.io, eosone.com  
**Created:** April 2026  
**Purpose:** How to run every EOS meeting type, including agendas, rules, and facilitation tips

---

## Why the Meeting Pulse Matters

Most business meetings are either too frequent (draining), too rare (disconnected), or too unstructured (wasteful). EOS replaces ad hoc meeting culture with a deliberate, predictable rhythm called the **Meeting Pulse**.

The Meeting Pulse does three things:
1. **Creates accountability** — Everyone knows when they'll be reporting on Rocks, Scorecard, and To-Dos
2. **Surfaces issues early** — Problems are caught weekly, not after they become crises
3. **Protects execution time** — Because issues have a designated time and place, people stop interrupting each other throughout the week

---

## The Four Meeting Types

| Meeting | Frequency | Duration | Participants |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Level 10 Meeting** | Weekly | 90 minutes | Leadership team |
| **Quarterly Planning** | Every 90 days | Full day (6–8 hours) | Leadership team |
| **Annual Planning** | Once per year | 2 full days | Leadership team |
| **Daily Huddle** | Daily (optional) | 10–15 minutes | Department teams |

## The Level 10 Meeting

### Purpose
The weekly heartbeat of EOS. Keeps the leadership team aligned, accountable, and solving issues before they compound.

### The Rules
- **Same day, same time, same location every week** — Non-negotiable
- **Start on time, end on time** — 90 minutes exactly
- **No devices** (or phone-down policy) — Full presence required
- **Fixed agenda** — Never changed or skipped; segments run in order
- **Issues go on the list** — Don't solve them in the wrong section

### The Fixed Agenda (90 Minutes)

**Segue / Good News — 5 minutes**  
Each person shares one personal and one professional win from the past week.  
*Purpose:* Transitions everyone mentally from execution mode to team mode. Builds human connection.

**Scorecard Review — 5 minutes**  
Each Scorecard metric is quickly reviewed: is it on-track (green) or off-track (red)?  
- Off-track items go to the Issues List — no discussion yet  
- No explanations in this section — just the number and the status  
*Purpose:* Removes opinion from business health; gives the team a data-driven weekly snapshot.

**Rock Review — 5 minutes**  
Each Rock owner reports: **On Track** or **Off Track**.  
- No updates, no status reports — just one of those two answers  
- Off-track Rocks go to the Issues List  
*Purpose:* Maintains 90-day priority focus without letting quarterly goals drift into background noise.

**Customer / Employee Headlines — 5 minutes**  
Each leader shares one positive and one negative headline about customers or employees.  
*Purpose:* Surfaces emerging issues in relationships before they become formal problems.

**To-Do List Review — 5 minutes**  
Every To-Do from the previous meeting is reviewed: **Done** or **Not Done**.  
- No partial credit — it's binary  
- Not Done items go to the Issues List  
*Purpose:* Creates a culture of follow-through; To-Dos are commitments, not suggestions.

**IDS — 60 minutes**  
The leadership team solves the top 3–5 issues on the Issues List using the Identify-Discuss-Solve process.  
*Facilitation:*  
1. Before IDS begins, everyone looks at the Issues List and adds any new items (1–2 minutes)
2. Prioritize the top 3 issues by quick vote or consensus (2 minutes)
3. IDS each issue: identify root cause → discuss → assign solution with owner and deadline
4. Move to the next issue once solved  
*Purpose:* This is the highest-value section. 60 minutes of focused problem-solving replaces hours of hallway conversations.

**Conclude — 5 minutes**  
- Review all new To-Dos assigned during IDS
- Confirm owners and deadlines
- Each person rates the meeting from 1–10
- Average the scores — if consistently below 8, discuss what would improve it  
*Purpose:* Builds accountability for follow-through and creates a feedback loop on meeting quality.

### Facilitation Tips
- Assign a meeting facilitator — typically the Integrator or the most disciplined member
- Use a timer for each section; move on when time is up
- "Parking lot" off-topic items — put them on the Issues List for a future meeting
- Keep a running Issues List visible (whiteboard, screen, or shared doc)
- If the meeting consistently scores below 8, IDS the meeting itself

## The Quarterly Planning Session

### Purpose
Step back from daily execution to evaluate progress, solve major issues, and realign for the next 90 days.

### Rules
- **Off-site preferred** — Physical separation from the office creates mental separation
- **Full day** — No half-day shortcuts; this is high-stakes leadership work
- **Attendance required** — No partial attendance from any leadership team member

### Full Agenda (6–8 hours)

**Segue — 10 minutes**  
Good news, personal check-in. Ground everyone for a full day of focused work.

**Previous Quarter Review — 30 minutes**  
- Financial performance vs. 1-Year Plan
- Rock completion (what % of Rocks were completed?)
- Scorecard trend review
- What went well? What didn't?
- No blame, just honest assessment

**Learning and Adjustment — 20 minutes**  
- For each Rock that was Off Track: what happened? What do we change?
- For each Rock that was On Track: what made it successful?

**V/TO Review — 30 minutes**  
- Is the Core Focus still right?
- Is the 10-Year Target still relevant?
- Is the 1-Year Plan still achievable? Do any targets need to change?
- Update as needed

**Issues Solving — 60 minutes**  
- Bring the biggest unresolved issues from the past quarter
- IDS each one
- This is the session's most valuable time — protect it

**Rock Setting — 90 minutes**  
*Company-level Rocks first:*  
1. Brainstorm: What are all the important things we could tackle?
2. Prioritize: Which 3–7 will move us closest to our 1-Year Plan?
3. Define each Rock clearly (owner + measurable outcome)
4. Assign to one person  

*Individual-level Rocks:*  
1. Each leader identifies their personal Rocks for the quarter
2. Review for alignment with company Rocks
3. Confirm no overlap or conflicts

**Cascading and Communication — 30 minutes**  
- How will company Rocks be communicated to the broader team?
- What do managers need to tell their teams?
- What (if anything) should be kept at leadership level for now?

**Close — 10 minutes**  
- Summarize all new To-Dos
- Rate the session 1–10
- Confirm next Quarterly date

## The Annual Planning Session (2 Days)

### Purpose
Zoom out fully. Revisit long-term vision, rebuild team health, and set the plan for the full year ahead.

### Rules
- **2 full days, off-site** — This cannot be compressed into one day
- **Day 1 is about the team and the vision**
- **Day 2 is about the plan**

### Day 1 Agenda

**Morning: Team Health (3–4 hours)**
- Open conversation: "What's working in how we operate as a leadership team?"
- "What's not working?"
- "What are we not talking about that we need to talk about?"
- Individual commitments: "One thing I will do differently this year to make our team stronger"
- Resolve lingering interpersonal or operational tensions

**Afternoon: Long-Term Vision (3–4 hours)**
- Review Core Values — are they still true?
- Review Core Focus — is the purpose and niche still right?
- Update or reaffirm 10-Year Target
- Build or update the 3-Year Picture (specific, tangible description of the company in 3 years)
- Review Marketing Strategy — target market, unique differentiator, proven process

### Day 2 Agenda

**Morning: 1-Year Plan (3 hours)**
- What is this year's revenue target?
- What is this year's profit target?
- What are the 3–7 most important measurable goals for the year?
- What is in the 1-Year Issues List?

**Afternoon: Q1 Rocks and Close (3 hours)**
- Set Q1 company Rocks
- Set individual Rocks for Q1
- Finalize updated V/TO
- Plan how to share the updated V/TO with the full organization
- Close with team commitments

## The Daily Huddle (Optional)

### Purpose
A brief daily check-in at the department level — not the leadership level — to surface daily blockers and coordinate execution.

### Format (10–15 minutes, standing)
1. What did you do yesterday? (1–2 sentences each)
2. What are you doing today?
3. What's in your way?

### Rules
- Keep it to 15 minutes maximum — strictly
- No problem-solving in the huddle; blockers go to the Issues List for IDS
- Standing meetings go faster — literally stand up
- Optional for leadership; recommended for operational departments

## Scorecard for Meeting Health

After each Level 10 Meeting, the team rates it 1–10. Track these scores quarterly.

| Average Score | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 8–10 | Meetings are running well; keep the rhythm |
| 6–7 | Something is off — IDS the meeting itself |
| Below 6 | Systemic problem — likely a facilitation or Issues List issue |

Common reasons for low scores:
- Too much time spent on updates, not enough on IDS
- Scorecard or Rock reviews turn into discussion (move it to IDS)
- Issues List is too long — needs prioritization discipline
- The facilitator isn't enforcing the agenda
- One person dominates the IDS discussion
