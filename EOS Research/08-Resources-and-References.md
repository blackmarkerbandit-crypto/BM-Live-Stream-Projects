## EOS Software Platforms

These tools are built specifically to run EOS — they replace spreadsheets for Rocks, Scorecards, Issues Lists, and Level 10 Meetings.

| Platform | Best For |
|---|---|
| **Ninety.io** | Full EOS platform; most widely used; integrates all tools |
| **Strety** | EOS-first platform with integrations (Slack, etc.) |
| **Traction Tools** | Original EOS software; built by an Implementer |
| **EOS One (app)** | Official EOS Worldwide app for tracking V/TO and Rocks |

**Note:** For small teams starting out, a shared Google Sheet or Notion board is sufficient. Move to dedicated software once the team hits 25+ people or the Level 10 Meetings become difficult to track manually.

## Complementary Frameworks and Books

These are not EOS but pair well with it and fill gaps in specific areas:

### Leadership and Team Health
- *The Five Dysfunctions of a Team* — Patrick Lencioni (team trust, conflict, commitment, accountability, results)
- *Radical Candor* — Kim Scott (feedback culture and direct communication)
- *The Advantage* — Patrick Lencioni (organizational health)

### Strategy
- *Good to Great* — Jim Collins (Level 5 Leadership, Hedgehog Concept maps to Core Focus)
- *Built to Last* — Collins & Porras (Core Values and Big Hairy Audacious Goals maps to 10-Year Target)
- *Blue Ocean Strategy* — Kim & Mauborgne (differentiation; maps to Marketing Strategy)

### Execution and Operations
- *The E-Myth Revisited* — Michael Gerber (working on vs. in the business; core to Process component)
- *Scaling Up* — Verne Harnish (Rockefeller Habits; complements EOS for larger companies)
- *The One Thing* — Gary Keller (focus and priorities; reinforces Rocks mindset)

### People and Hiring
- *Who: The A Method for Hiring* — Geoff Smart & Randy Street (rigorous hiring process)
- *First, Break All the Rules* — Buckingham & Coffman (strengths-based management; supports GWC)
- *The Ideal Team Player* — Patrick Lencioni (humble, hungry, smart — maps to People Analyzer)

### Finance
- *Profit First* — Mike Michalowicz (cash management system; pairs with EOS Scorecard)
- *Simple Numbers* — Greg Crabtree (financial clarity for entrepreneurs)

## Key Terminology Quick Reference

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **EOS** | Entrepreneurial Operating System — the full framework |
| **V/TO** | Vision/Traction Organizer — 2-page strategic plan document |
| **Rocks** | Top 3–7 priorities for the next 90 days |
| **Level 10 Meeting (L10)** | Weekly 90-minute structured team meeting |
| **Scorecard** | 5–15 weekly metrics tracking business health |
| **IDS** | Identify, Discuss, Solve — the EOS problem-solving method |
| **People Analyzer** | Tool to assess Core Values fit and GWC fit |
| **GWC** | Get it / Want it / Capacity to do it |
| **Accountability Chart** | Seat-based org structure with defined accountabilities |
| **Visionary** | Big-picture creative leader (often founder) |
| **Integrator** | Execution-focused leader who runs the business |
| **Core Focus** | The company's purpose/cause/passion + its niche |
| **Core Values** | 3–5 non-negotiable behavioral standards defining culture |
| **10-Year Target** | Long-term big goal (specific and measurable) |
| **3-Year Picture** | Concrete description of the company in 3 years |
| **Meeting Pulse** | The structured cadence of weekly, quarterly, and annual meetings |
| **Issues List** | Running prioritized list of problems, ideas, and decisions |
| **Core Processes** | The 5–7 documented workflows that define how the business runs |
| **3-Strike Rule** | Framework for addressing performance issues before a people decision |
| **Delegate and Elevate** | Tool for leaders to identify what to hand off |
| **Clarity Break** | Protected solo thinking time for leaders to work on the business |
| **Following by All** | Verification that a core process is understood and adopted by the team |
| **Traction Library** | The series of EOS books by Wickman and co-authors |
| **EOS Implementer** | Certified facilitator who guides companies through EOS adoption |
| **Fractional Integrator** | Part-time or contract Integrator for companies not ready for a full hire |

## EOS Implementation Checklist

Use this to track readiness at each phase:

### Foundation (Months 1–3)
- [ ] Leadership team has read *Traction*
- [ ] Organizational Checkup completed (baseline score)
- [ ] Accountability Chart drafted
- [ ] Core Values defined (3–5 values with behavioral examples)
- [ ] Core Focus defined (purpose + niche)
- [ ] First V/TO draft completed
- [ ] Level 10 Meetings launched (weekly, fixed agenda)
- [ ] First company Rocks set (Q1)
- [ ] Scorecard built with 5–10 metrics and owners

### Strengthening (Months 4–12)
- [ ] First Quarterly Planning session completed
- [ ] People Analyzer run on all leadership team members
- [ ] Accountability Chart revised based on reality
- [ ] GWC applied to all current seats
- [ ] Issues List actively used and updated weekly
- [ ] IDS process smooth in Level 10 Meetings
- [ ] 3–5 core processes identified
- [ ] At least 2 core processes documented
- [ ] Core processes reviewed with team ("Followed by All")

### Mastery (Months 13–24)
- [ ] Rock completion rate consistently 80%+
- [ ] All 6 core processes documented and followed
- [ ] Level 10 Meetings consistently score 8+ out of 10
- [ ] People Analyzer used in every hiring decision
- [ ] Departmental Level 10 Meetings launched
- [ ] V/TO shared and understood organization-wide
- [ ] Visionary-Integrator dynamic clearly defined and functioning
- [ ] Annual Planning session completed
- [ ] Organizational Checkup re-taken — score compared to baseline
