# Monetization & Ticketing for Venue Streaming

## Revenue Models

### 1. Pay-Per-View (PPV) Ticketed Streams

Charge viewers a one-time fee to access a specific live stream event.

**How it works:** Viewers purchase a ticket (usually $5-50) and receive a unique link or access code for the stream. Access is typically available for the live event plus a limited replay window (24-72 hours).

**Best for:** Headline concerts, exclusive performances, premium events, theater productions, film screenings.

**Platforms:**
| Platform | Transaction Fee | Features | Best For |
|----------|----------------|----------|----------|
| **Mandolin** | ~15-20% | Music-focused, merch integration, tipping | Live music venues |
| **Eventive** | ~10% + $150/event | Film screening tools, Q&A | Film festivals, arts |
| **Staged** | ~15% | Theater-focused, programs, intermission | Performing arts |
| **Vimeo OTT** | Custom pricing | White-label, your branding | Custom branded experience |
| **Looped** | ~10-15% | Hybrid ticketing (in-person + virtual) | Venues selling both ticket types |
| **YouTube Paid Events** | 30% | Built into YouTube | Existing YouTube audience |

**Pricing guidance:**
- Set virtual tickets at **30-50% of in-person ticket price** as a baseline
- Premium events with exclusive content can command higher pricing
- Bundle virtual tickets with merch or VOD access for perceived value
- Consider early-bird pricing for virtual tickets

### 2. Subscription / Membership Model

Viewers pay a recurring fee for access to all your streamed content.

**How it works:** Monthly or annual subscription grants access to all live streams and VOD library. Think of it as a "virtual season pass."

**Best for:** Venues with consistent weekly/monthly programming (worship services, comedy clubs, jazz clubs, lecture series).

**Platforms:**
- **Patreon** — Creator-focused, tiered memberships, community tools
- **YouTube Memberships** — Integrated with YouTube; perks like badges, emotes, early access
- **Vimeo OTT** — Build your own subscription streaming service
- **Memberful** — Integrates with your website for custom membership gates

**Pricing guidance:**
- $5-10/month for regular programming
- $15-25/month for premium content + exclusive perks
- Offer annual plans at a 15-20% discount to reduce churn

### 3. Tipping / Donations

Viewers send voluntary payments during or after a stream.

**How it works:** Viewers click a button to send a one-time payment, often with a message that appears on screen.

**Best for:** Music performances, worship services, charity events, community shows.

**Platforms:**
- **YouTube Super Chat** — Messages highlighted in chat; 30% YouTube cut
- **Twitch Bits** — Virtual currency; viewers buy bits, streamer redeems; ~30% Twitch cut
- **Facebook Stars** — Similar to Bits; $0.01 per Star to creator
- **Streamlabs** — Direct tipping with on-screen alerts; minimal fees (payment processor only)
- **Ko-fi** — Simple tip jar; 0% platform fee (just payment processor)
- **Venmo/Cash App QR** — Display QR code on stream; zero fees; no platform integration

**Maximizing tips:**
- Display a tip/donate link or QR code on screen throughout the stream
- Use Streamlabs or StreamElements alerts to show tips on screen in real time (social proof)
- Acknowledge tippers by name (builds culture of generosity)
- Set suggested amounts ($5, $10, $25) rather than open-ended

### 4. Sponsorship & Advertising

Sell sponsorships or run ads on your stream.

**Approaches:**
| Type | Description | Revenue Potential |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Pre-roll sponsor card** | 15-30 second branded graphic before the show | $200-2,000/event |
| **Lower third sponsor bug** | Logo displayed during stream | $100-500/event |
| **Branded segments** | "This intermission brought to you by..." | $500-2,000/event |
| **Product placement** | Sponsor products visible on stage/bar | Varies |
| **Platform ads (YouTube)** | YouTube runs ads; you earn revenue share | $2-10 per 1,000 views |

**Tips for venues:**
- Local businesses (restaurants, breweries, hotels) near your venue are natural sponsors
- Offer sponsors a package: logo on stream + social media mentions + in-venue signage
- Track and report viewer numbers to demonstrate value to sponsors

### 5. Hybrid Ticketing (In-Person + Virtual)

Sell both physical and virtual tickets to the same event.

**How it works:** Your ticketing platform offers two ticket types. Virtual ticket holders access the stream; in-person ticket holders attend physically. Some platforms offer "upgrade" options (virtual → in-person or vice versa).

**Platforms:**
- **Eventbrite + streaming platform** — Eventbrite for in-person, separate platform for virtual
- **Looped** — Single platform for both ticket types
- **Universe (Ticketmaster)** — Enterprise hybrid events
- **Custom website** — Embed stream player behind a login gate

**Considerations:**
- Virtual tickets should never undercut in-person ticket value — the in-person experience is premium
- Offer virtual-only perks (exclusive camera angles, backstage streams, artist chat)
- Track virtual attendance separately for reporting and sponsor value

### 6. VOD (Video on Demand) Revenue

Monetize recordings of past streams.

**Options:**
- **YouTube ad revenue** — Upload VODs; earn from ads (requires YouTube Partner Program)
- **Paid VOD access** — Sell replay access for 48-72 hours after an event
- **Subscription library** — Include VODs in your membership offering
- **Licensing** — License recordings to broadcasters, documentarians, or artists

## Revenue Comparison by Model

| Model | Setup Effort | Recurring Revenue | Per-Event Revenue | Best Audience Size |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|--------------------|
| PPV Tickets | Moderate | No | High ($5-50/viewer) | 50-5,000 viewers |
| Subscriptions | High (ongoing content needed) | Yes | Moderate | 100+ regular viewers |
| Tipping | Low | Variable | Low-Moderate ($1-10/tipper) | Any |
| Sponsorships | Moderate (sales effort) | Possible | Moderate-High | 200+ viewers |
| Hybrid Ticketing | Moderate | No | Moderate | 50-5,000 viewers |
| VOD | Low | Possible | Low | Large back catalog |

## Getting Started: A Practical Path

1. **Start with tipping** — add a Streamlabs/Ko-fi link to your streams immediately. Zero risk, minimal setup.
2. **Add sponsorships** — approach 2-3 local businesses once you have consistent viewer numbers to report.
3. **Trial PPV** — pick one premium event and sell virtual tickets. Measure demand.
4. **Consider subscriptions** — only when you have consistent programming (weekly+) and demonstrated audience retention.

## Payment Processing Notes

- Most platforms handle payment processing (Stripe, PayPal) and issue payouts on a schedule
- You'll need a business bank account and tax ID for most platforms
- Track all streaming revenue for tax purposes — it's taxable income
- International viewers may face currency conversion fees — note this in your ticketing FAQ
