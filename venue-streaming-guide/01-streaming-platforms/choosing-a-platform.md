# Choosing the Right Streaming Platform for Your Venue

## Step 1: Define Your Goals

Before evaluating platforms, answer these questions:

1. **What are you streaming?** Live music, conferences, worship services, comedy, sports, theater?
2. **Who is your audience?** Local community, global fans, corporate attendees, paying ticket holders?
3. **How do you want to make money?** Ad revenue, ticket sales, sponsorships, tips/donations, or simply marketing the venue?
4. **How often will you stream?** One-off events, weekly series, or daily programming?
5. **What level of production quality do you need?** Single camera with house audio, or multi-cam broadcast-quality?

## Step 2: Match Goals to Platform Strengths

### "We want to reach as many people as possible for free"
**Go with:** YouTube Live + Facebook Live (simultaneously via Restream)

YouTube gives you global search discoverability and permanent VOD archives. Facebook targets your existing local community. Running both costs nothing and doubles your reach.

### "We want to sell tickets to virtual attendees"
**Go with:** A dedicated ticketed streaming platform

- **Eventive** — Popular for film festivals and arts venues
- **Mandolin** — Built specifically for live music venues
- **Vimeo OTT** — White-label pay-per-view with your branding
- **Staged** — Designed for theater and performing arts
- **Looped** — Hybrid event ticketing with streaming built in

These platforms handle payment processing, access control, and often provide embeddable players for your website.

### "We stream weekly and want to build a loyal community"
**Go with:** Twitch or YouTube Live

Both platforms reward consistency. Twitch's subscription model and chat culture build tight communities. YouTube's algorithm surfaces channels that stream regularly.

### "We need a polished, branded experience for corporate clients"
**Go with:** Vimeo Livestream or StreamYard Pro

Ad-free players, custom branding, password protection, and embeddable widgets. Corporate clients expect a premium look without platform branding.

### "We host hybrid events (in-person + remote)"
**Go with:** A combination approach

- Ticketing platform for paid virtual access (Eventive, Mandolin)
- YouTube/Facebook simulcast for free promotional clips or after-shows
- Your own website as the central hub with embedded player

## Step 3: Technical Requirements Checklist

Before committing to a platform, verify:

- [ ] **Ingest protocol support** — Does it accept RTMP, SRT, or RTSP from your encoder?
- [ ] **Resolution and bitrate limits** — Can it handle your target quality (1080p60 minimum for music)?
- [ ] **Stream key management** — Can you set persistent keys or do they rotate per stream?
- [ ] **Scheduling and promotion** — Can you schedule streams in advance with thumbnails and descriptions?
- [ ] **Chat and moderation** — Does it offer chat, Q&A, polls, or moderation tools you need?
- [ ] **Analytics** — Can you see concurrent viewers, peak viewership, geographic data, and watch time?
- [ ] **VOD and archiving** — Are recordings automatically saved? For how long? Can you download them?
- [ ] **API access** — If you plan to automate, does the platform offer APIs for scheduling and analytics?

## Step 4: Start Simple, Scale Up

A common mistake is over-investing in platforms before validating demand. A practical rollout:

1. **Month 1:** Stream to YouTube Live using OBS (free). Test your setup, learn what works.
2. **Month 2:** Add Facebook Live via Restream. Compare audience engagement across platforms.
3. **Month 3:** If ticket sales are a goal, trial a paid platform for one premium event.
4. **Ongoing:** Let data guide where to invest. Double down on the platform where your audience actually shows up.
