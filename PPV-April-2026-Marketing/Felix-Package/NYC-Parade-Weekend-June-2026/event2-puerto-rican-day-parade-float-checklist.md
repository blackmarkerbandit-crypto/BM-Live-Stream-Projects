## PART 1: FLOAT APPLICATION & COMPLIANCE

### Application — COMPLETE

### Insurance (Required)
- [ ] Obtain $1,000,000+ general liability insurance certificate
  - **Estimated cost: $300–$800** depending on coverage scope and provider
  - **Fastest options:**
    - **Thimble** (thimble.com) — event GL by the day; ~$100–$300/day; get a quote online in minutes
    - **NEXT Insurance** (nextinsurance.com) — instant online quotes; typically $300–$800 for short-term event policy
    - **Your existing business broker** — adding an event rider may be cheaper than a standalone policy
    - **Ask NPRDP at orientation** — they deal with this every year and may have a preferred vendor or group rate
  - Coverage scope to clarify: organization only, or include talent + 50-person walking contingent?
  - Two back-to-back event days (June 13–14) — confirm if one policy covers both
- [ ] Name "National Puerto Rican Day Parade, Inc." as additional insured on the certificate (standard request, no extra cost)
- [ ] Submit certificate to parade organizers per their deadline
- [ ] Note: NPRDP Custom Float package includes vehicle driver insurance — this GL is for your organization/event separately

### Walking Contingent Compliance Rules
- [ ] Maximum 50 participants per contingent — already at the limit, do not exceed
- [ ] Each contingent must designate a **Marshall** (usually the application contact) to ensure safety on the route
- [ ] NPRDP provides permits and wristbands upon application completion — **no contingent can march without wristbands**
- [ ] **NO third-parties** may march with the registered group — all 50 walkers must be part of BMB/BlackMarker.TV's registered contingent
- [ ] All participants must identify with or respect Puerto Rican heritage and culture
- [ ] Dress code: reflects respect for public decency; Puerto Rican flags must be used respectfully and in dignified manner
- [ ] Flags with symbols not allowed (per NPRDP rules — confirm current guidelines)

### Float Safety Compliance
- [ ] All structural elements must be secured — nothing can detach from the float during the route
- [ ] Float max dimensions: **12 ft height × 20 ft length × 8 ft width** (per NPRDP specs)
- [ ] Street clearance: minimum 12 inches above ground
- [ ] Generator must be enclosed/sound-dampened and strapped down
- [ ] All electrical wiring must be properly insulated and managed
- [ ] Float will be inspected at staging area day of — have all permits and insurance docs on hand
- [ ] If using NPRDP float package: driver and vehicle insurance is included — confirm inspection process with organizers

## PART 3: POWER SETUP (ON FLOAT)

- [ ] Generator: Honda EU7000iS (7,000W inverter) — OR two Honda EU3000iS in parallel
- [ ] Load estimate: DJ + PA (~3,500W) + stream tech (~400W) + lights (~800W) + misc (~300W) = ~5,000W peak
- [ ] Generator secured to float with steel straps / tie-down chains — must not move during route
- [ ] Exhaust pipe: positioned away from talent and walking contingent (rear of float, away from crowd)
- [ ] Generator fuel: full tank before staging + 5-gallon backup can (secured in approved container)
- [ ] Fuel management: at 50% load, EU7000iS runs ~6 hours — parade is 3–4 hours; should be fine
- [ ] Have designated crew member check generator every 45 minutes during route
- [ ] All wiring on float: secured with cable trays or secured to structure — no loose cables on float surface

## PART 4: LIVE STREAM TECH SETUP (MOVING VEHICLE)

### Connectivity (Most Critical — Moving Vehicle)
- [ ] **Must use cellular bonding** — WiFi is impossible; single hotspot will drop
- [ ] LiveU Solo+ or Teradek Bond: 3–4 SIM cards bonded (AT&T + T-Mobile + Verizon + optional international SIM)
- [ ] Mount bonding device securely on float (vibration-resistant position)
- [ ] Antennas pointed upward for best signal (not blocked by float structure)
- [ ] Test connectivity while moving in a vehicle before parade day
- [ ] Target: 8–15 Mbps stable; stream at 6 Mbps 1080p30

### Cameras
- [ ] Primary camera: mounted on vibration-dampening arm/mount — framing Joselito + Host 1 with street/crowd behind
- [ ] Mount position: stable, secure, weatherproof — camera cannot fall during route
- [ ] Vibration dampening: rubber isolation mount between float structure and camera arm
- [ ] Field camera (movearound): carried by Host 2 walking in contingent OR riding float and leaning out
- [ ] GoPro Hero 12 on chest harness as additional B-roll/backup angle (optional)
- [ ] Test camera mounts before event day — drive test in a vehicle to confirm stability

### Audio
- [ ] Rode Wireless GO II: Joselito mic + Host 1 mic
- [ ] Wind protection: furry windscreens (deadcat) — outdoor + moving vehicle = serious wind noise
- [ ] DJ audio: direct line out from DJ mixer → DI box → encoder
- [ ] IEM monitoring: both hosts hear each other + program audio (critical in open-air moving environment)
- [ ] Sound check before parade starts at staging area
- [ ] Monitor DJ speaker levels: parade regulations often limit sound levels — check with organizers

### Production
- [ ] vMix on production laptop (secured on float tech station)
- [ ] Laptop secured: laptop lock or strap to table; must not shift if float hits a bump
- [ ] Stream keys configured: BlackMarker.TV + YouTube + Facebook
- [ ] Local recording enabled (safety net)
- [ ] Graphics preloaded: "LIVE — PUERTO RICAN DAY PARADE" lower third, talent name cards, bumper
- [ ] Test stream from staging area 1 hour before parade begins

## PART 6: DJ CHARLIE CHASE — COORDINATION

### Background (Host Talking Points)
- Real name: Carlos Mendes | Born January 16, 1959, Manhattan (Puerto Rican parents)
- Co-founded Cold Crush Brothers in **1975** with DJ Tony Tony — first hip-hop group signed by CBS Records
- One of the first prominent Latino DJs in hip-hop; overcame initial exclusion because hip-hop was perceived as "a Black thing"
- Pioneered mixing salsa beats into hip-hop parties — literally fusing Puerto Rican and Black musical culture
- Inducted into the **Technics DMC DJ Hall of Fame in 2003**
- **He is a historical honoree on the NPRDP website** — perfect alignment with "Celebrating Puerto Rican Pioneers" theme
- Bridging Puerto Rican culture and the birth of hip-hop in the Bronx

## PART 9: RISK MANAGEMENT

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Float application deadline missed | **Check immediately** — this is the highest-risk item for this event |
| Insurance not secured | Apply through a broker specializing in event/entertainment insurance |
| Generator failure mid-route | Have a 300W+ battery bank (Jackery/EcoFlow) as emergency backup for laptops |
| Camera mount failing on bumps | Test mount in a moving vehicle before parade day; have secondary mount ready |
| Cellular drop in parade congestion | 3-carrier bonding; lower stream bitrate to 4 Mbps if stability is an issue |
| Walker drops out last minute | Have 5 spare shirts; maintain a short waitlist of 5–10 people who want to walk |
| Float inspection failure | Know the requirements in advance; pre-check all structural/electrical elements day before |
| Rain | Equipment on float in Pelican cases until needed; rain covers for all exposed gear; Charlie Chase and Joselito have shelter plan |
| Driver issue | Have backup driver contact on standby; confirm driver license and experience before event day |
