## PART 2: KEY RESEARCH FINDINGS

### 116th Street Festival — Logistics & Requirements

**Permits & Applications:**
- Vendor participation requires application through the festival organizers (typically El Museo del Barrio / 116th Street Business Improvement District or associated cultural organizations)
- NYC Parks Department and Department of Transportation issue street closure permits — handled by the organizer, but vendors must comply with their specific placement rules
- Apply early: applications typically open 2–4 months before the festival
- Action item: Contact the 116th Street Business Improvement District and/or El Museo del Barrio directly to confirm vendor application status

**Tent & Space Requirements (NYC Street Festivals):**
- Standard vendor space: 10x10 or 10x20 ft
- Tent/canopy required — must be fire-retardant certified (NYC requirement for commercial events)
- All tent legs must be weighted — NYC requires minimum 40–50 lbs per leg (sandbags or water weights)
- No staking into asphalt permitted
- Sidewalls recommended for tech equipment protection and wind management
- Generator use: permitted but must be properly vented; caged/enclosed generators preferred

**Power:**
- Vendors typically provide their own power via generator
- Some festivals offer electrical hookup for a fee — confirm with organizers
- For live streaming + DJ + commentary setup: minimum 3,500W recommended; 5,000W+ gives headroom

**Crowd/Noise:**
- High ambient noise environment — critical for commentary mic setup
- Directional microphones or broadcast headsets with noise cancellation required
- Monitor mix for hosts (IEMs) so they can hear the show in the noise

### National Puerto Rican Day Parade — Float Requirements

**Organizer:** National Puerto Rican Day Parade, Inc.
- Application process opens typically in fall/winter for the following June parade
- Registration fee varies by category (cultural float, corporate float, media float)
- All float operators must carry proof of $1,000,000+ general liability insurance
- Float must pass safety inspection on parade day (day-of check at staging area)

**Float Dimensions (standard guidelines):**
- Maximum length: approximately 40 feet (varies by permit/category — confirm with NPRDP Inc.)
- Maximum width: 8.5 feet (standard trailer width to fit in lane)
- Height: must clear signage and overhead elements along the route — generally under 13–14 feet to be safe
- Must be mounted on a licensed flatbed trailer with a registered vehicle

**Generator on Float:**
- Must be enclosed or sound-dampened to meet noise ordinances
- Must be secured to the float structure — NYC requires tie-down compliance
- Fuel: bring extra cans (secured properly); generator should be able to run 6–8 hours
- Keep generator away from talent and crowd-facing areas

**Walking Contingent:**
- 50-person walking contingent is standard and permissible
- All walkers should be registered with your float entry
- Matching branded shirts/uniforms required for cohesive look and crowd recognition
- Brief all walkers on route, pacing, and prohibited behaviors (no alcohol, no leaving the line)
- Designate a walking contingent captain — someone who manages the group independent of the float

**Music & DJ:**
- DJ on float is permitted; PA speakers must face outward (toward spectators)
- Sound level restrictions enforced along the route — have a sound level plan
- Music should be curated playlist + live DJ interaction; avoid dead air

**Route & Logistics:**
- Staging area: floats typically stage in the 40s on Fifth Avenue, hours before parade start
- Route: 44th Street up Fifth Avenue to 79th Street (approximately 1.8 miles)
- Parade duration: 3–4 hours for the full route at parade pace
- Have a float driver who knows the route and has operated vehicles in large parade settings
- No stopping permitted in the middle of the route

### DJ Charlie Chase — Background & Cultural Significance

DJ Charlie Chase (real name Carlos Mendes) is a foundational figure in hip-hop and Puerto Rican culture:

- **Origins:** Born January 16, 1959 in Manhattan to Puerto Rican parents
- **Hip-Hop Pioneer:** Co-founded the Cold Crush Brothers in **1975** with DJ Tony Tone — one of the most legendary hip-hop crews in history; first hip-hop group signed by CBS Records
- **Cultural Bridge:** One of the first prominent Latino DJs in hip-hop; initially excluded because hip-hop was perceived as "a Black thing" — he broke through and pioneered mixing salsa beats into hip-hop sets, fusing Puerto Rican and Black musical culture
- **Recognition:** Inducted into the **Technics DMC DJ Hall of Fame in 2003**
- **NPRDP Connection:** He is a **historical honoree on nprdpinc.org** — this booking is perfectly aligned with both the parade organization and the theme
- **Significance to This Theme:** "Celebrating Puerto Rican Pioneers" — Chase helped build hip-hop from the ground up while carrying Puerto Rican identity in the Bronx

**Talking Points for Broadcast:**
- "Before the genre had a name, Charlie Chase was building it in the Bronx"
- Puerto Rican identity as central to hip-hop's origin story — not just participants, but architects
- Joselito DaPuppet + Charlie Chase = two generations of Puerto Rican entertainment excellence on one float

### Times Square Billboard Advertising

**How It Works:**
- Digital billboard space is sold through major OOH companies: Lamar Advertising, Clear Channel Outdoor, Outfront Media, Intersection (NYC-specific)
- Times Square boards are some of the most in-demand/expensive OOH in the US
- Ads run in rotation — typically 8–10 second spots in a 60–90 second loop
- Minimum buys vary by vendor and board; some require 2–4 week minimums

**Confirmed Pricing (2026):**
| Board Type | Approximate Cost |
|---|---|
| Standard 15-second spot (per day) | $300–$3,000/day |
| Standard 30-second spot (per day) | $500–$5,000/day |
| Weekly (standard placement) | $20,000–$50,000+/week |
| 4-week standard (non-prime screens) | ~$50,000 for 15-second spot |
| Premium locations (Marriott Marquis, etc.) | $300,000+/4 weeks |
| Secondary / adjacent Times Square boards | $3,000–$8,000/week |

**Important — June is Peak Season:**
June–August is the summer tourism peak for Times Square advertising — prices are at their highest. February–May and September–October offer the best rates and easiest availability. For a June parade date, you are paying peak rates.

**Practical Path for BMB Budget:**
- Target secondary/adjacent boards rather than marquee Times Square — same "Times Square billboard" story at a fraction of the cost
- Look for 15-second rotating spot packages through Outfront Media, Clear Channel Outdoor, or theadfocus.com
- The narrative is: "Our talent on a Times Square billboard during Puerto Rican Heritage Month" — a $3–5K secondary board delivers that story
- Book 2–4 weeks in advance (standard lead time)
- Creative specs: high-resolution digital file, minimum 1080p — verify exact specs with booking company

## PART 3: LIVE STREAMING IMPLEMENTATION ANALYSIS

### The Challenge Landscape

Both events present unique live streaming challenges that require different solutions:

| Factor | 116th St Festival (Tent) | Parade Float |
|---|---|---|
| Location | Fixed tent position | Moving vehicle |
| Internet | Stationary — 4G/5G bonding | Mobile — 4G/5G bonding (moving) |
| Power | Generator at tent | Generator on float |
| Audio | High crowd noise, stationary mics | Open air, moving, wind |
| Camera | Fixed commentary + roaming | Fixed float cam + roaming |
| Duration | 4–6 hours continuous | 3–4 hours (parade route) |
| Risk | Equipment theft, heat | Road vibration, wind, motion |

---

### Internet Connectivity — Most Critical Factor

**Do NOT rely on venue WiFi or a single hotspot.** Both events require cellular bonding.

**Recommended Setup:**
- **LiveU Solo+ or Teradek Prism Flex** — hardware cellular bonding devices that combine 2–4 SIM cards (AT&T + T-Mobile + Verizon) for a stable, redundant stream
- Alternatively: **Peplink MAX BR1 MK2** or **Mushroom Networks Truffle** for bonded cellular
- Budget: $800–$2,500 to rent for the weekend (or purchase LiveU Solo+ at ~$3,000)
- Target upload speed: minimum 8–10 Mbps stable; bonded cellular in NYC can achieve 20–50 Mbps
- Bring SIM cards from all three major carriers — carrier redundancy is insurance

**NYC-Specific Note:** Midtown Manhattan and East Harlem (116th St) have excellent 5G coverage from all carriers. The parade route on Fifth Avenue has heavy network congestion on parade day — bonding across carriers is essential, not optional.

---

### Power — Generator Specs

**Event 1 (Festival Tent):**
- Load calculation: DJ setup (~1,200W) + streaming encoder/laptop (~300W) + monitors/displays (~400W) + lights/signage (~500W) + misc (~300W) = ~2,700W peak
- **Recommended: Honda EU3000iS** (3,000W inverter generator) — runs at ~57dB, suitable for tent environment
- Alternatively: 3,500W inverter generator from Cat or Generac
- Fuel: 5-gallon capacity runs ~8 hours at 50% load; bring 5-gallon backup can
- Run generator at least 15 feet from the back of the tent if possible

**Event 2 (Parade Float):**
- Load calculation: DJ setup (~1,500W) + PA speakers on float (~2,000W) + streaming encoder (~300W) + display/branding lights (~800W) + misc (~400W) = ~5,000W peak
- **Recommended: Honda EU7000iS** (7,000W inverter generator) or two Honda EU3000iS units in parallel
- Float generators must be secured with straps/mounts and positioned away from talent
- Exhaust routing: ensure exhaust faces away from DJ, hosts, and walking contingent
- Fuel management: 5.1-gallon tank on EU7000iS = ~6 hours at 50% load; fill before staging

### Audio Setup

**Commentary Microphones:**
- **Rode Wireless GO II** (2-person system) — compact, wireless, reliable in crowd settings
- Foam windscreens on all outdoor mics — required
- IEM monitoring for both hosts (Shure SE215 + wireless transmitter) so they can hear each other and program audio in the crowd noise
- Backup: wired XLR headset mic as failsafe if wireless drops

**DJ Audio to Stream:**
- Take a direct line out from the DJ mixer to the streaming encoder — never mic the PA speakers
- Use a DI box (Radial ProDI) between mixer and encoder for clean signal
- Monitor DJ levels separately from commentary levels in the production software

**Ambient/Crowd Audio:**
- Add a wide-pattern stereo condenser (e.g., Zoom H6 in XY mode) near the front of the tent/float for crowd atmosphere
- Blend at about 20–30% in mix — enough to give the stream a "live feel" without overwhelming commentary

### Risk Management — Live Streaming Outdoors

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Cellular overload on parade day | Bond 3+ carriers; have backpack-style LiveU or Teradek |
| Rain/weather | Pelican cases for all equipment; canopy over tech station; rain covers for cameras |
| Generator failure | Bring backup power strip with surge protection; have spare fuel; carry 300W battery bank for laptops/phones |
| Audio drop | Wired backup mics; always have XLR cables as failsafe |
| Equipment theft (festival) | Never leave equipment unattended; lock cases when not in use; designate a "security person" at the tent at all times |
| Float vibration causing camera shake | Vibration-dampening mount; use electronic IS on cameras |
| Stream going down | Pre-built offline recording (vMix always records locally); notify audience on social and restart quickly |
