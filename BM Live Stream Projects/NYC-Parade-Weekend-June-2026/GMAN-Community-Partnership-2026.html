Black Marker TV is producing a full live-streamed media weekend for the 2026 National Puerto Rican Day Parade in New York City — anchored by a custom parade float, a vendor presence at the 116th Street Festival in East Harlem, and a full broadcast production team. The contingent theme: "BlackMarker.TV — Celebrating Puerto Rican Pioneers."

GMAN USA is the official clothing partner of the BlackMarker.TV walking contingent — supplying the 54 shirts worn by marchers through the streets of New York City on parade day. That's 54 walking billboards, on one of the most-watched Puerto Rican cultural events in the country, on a platform that is already telling that story. GMAN is not a sponsor — they are a community partner who showed up for their people.

The Weekend
Event 01
116th Street Festival
June 13, 2026 · East Harlem, NYC
Full vendor tent presence on 116th Street. Live broadcast on BlackMarker.TV. Joselito Da Puppet, DJ and host talent. Street-level activation in the heart of El Barrio.
Event 02
National Puerto Rican Day Parade
June 14, 2026 · 5th Avenue, NYC
Custom 8' × 28' × 13' parade float with full vinyl graphics, sound system, and live coverage. 54-person walking contingent. Broadcast on BlackMarker.TV. Talent: DJ Charlie Chase · Joselito Da Puppet · Thirstin Howl the 3rd.

Why This Makes Sense for GMAN

GMAN's tagline is "Make Your Mark. TODAY." This weekend, that mark is literal — 54 shirts, every one of them with the GMAN name on it, walking through the streets of New York City at one of the largest Puerto Rican cultural events in the world. That's not advertising. That's presence.

The Puerto Rican community in New York City is one of the most brand-loyal, community-driven markets in the country. They support brands that support them. GMAN isn't writing a check to buy space — they're showing up as a community partner that clothed the contingent, stood with the culture, and made the march possible. That story gets told on air, on social, on the float, and on every shirt that walks that route.

This is the kind of visibility that can't be bought with a banner ad. It's earned — and it's exactly what "Make Your Mark" means.
