### Event 1 Audience — DUH COMEDY SHOW (Los Diggity + Joselito DaPuppet & Friends)

**Who they are:**
- Latino/Hispanic community — heavily Puerto Rican, especially in the Tampa/Wesley Chapel corridor (one of the largest Puerto Rican populations in Florida)
- Multi-generational — Joselito DaPuppet's puppet family (Papi + Abuela) plays to ALL ages simultaneously
- Fans of relatable, culturally specific "Splangish" comedy (Spanish + English) who see themselves in the characters
- Social media-native audience — Joselito has 500K+ followers, 2M+ weekly reach across Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube
- People who love interactive live events — the live chat WITH comedians on stage is a genuine differentiator, not just a feature

**Where they live online:**
- Instagram (primary — his viral clips live here)
- TikTok (younger end of the fanbase)
- YouTube (longer-form content, comedy fans)
- Facebook (older Latino community, 35+)

**What drives them to buy:**
- FOMO — "los que no están ahí se van a perder" (those who aren't there are gonna miss it)
- Cultural pride — supporting Latino artists doing big things
- The novelty of chatting WITH the comedians live during the show
- It's interactive TV they can't get anywhere else

**Key tone:** Fun, bilingual-friendly, heavy on personality, community feel, exclamation points earned

### Event 2 Audience — DJ Frankie Cutlass + Hanson Freestyle Event (April 25)

**Who they are:**
- Latin/Hispanic adults 35–55 — the generation that grew up on freestyle in the late 80s/early 90s
- Predominantly Puerto Rican, Dominican, Cuban, and Italian-American communities (the exact communities freestyle was born in — NYC, NJ, now heavily settled in Florida)
- DJ Frankie Cutlass has 465,000+ Facebook likes — this audience lives on Facebook
- Grammy-nominated artist + Hanson's "Hungry for Your Love" = serious freestyle credibility for this crowd
- Tampa Bay area freestyle fans are PROVEN and ACTIVE — Amalie Arena sold out the 2024 Freestyle Explosion Throwback Jam. This audience shows up.
- 2,000+ people expected IN PERSON — that number is itself a marketing tool for the PPV audience

**Where they live online:**
- Facebook (primary — 35-55 demographic, community groups, event sharing)
- Instagram (secondary — catching up, but still active)
- YouTube (for music discovery, throwback mixes, DJ sets)

**What drives them to buy:**
- Nostalgia — this music defined their youth and they feel it viscerally
- FOMO at scale — 2,000 people in the building is a signal that something real is happening
- Accessibility — "I can't drive to Wesley Chapel but I can watch live from my couch"
- Frankie Cutlass name recognition — he's a brand to this audience

**Key tone:** Nostalgic, celebratory, old-school credibility, "throwback vibes," community gathering energy
