## The Hard Truths This Data Shows

### 1. Single-client dependency is the #1 business risk
Studio Enterprise represents 74% of total revenue. If they reduce booking frequency, change vendors, or pause for a semester — BMB loses the majority of its income overnight. This is not a business yet; it's a staffing arrangement with one client dressed up as a business.

**The fix:** Studio Enterprise needs to become one of many recurring revenue relationships — not the foundation the whole operation stands on.

### 2. IDVES is real money but structurally broken
$12,300/year from a client who books irregularly is better than nothing, but it creates planning problems. You can't staff, schedule, or invest based on income you can't predict. There is no retainer, no committed schedule, no reason for the client to book consistently.

**The fix:** Propose a service retainer to IDVES — guaranteed availability and priority scheduling in exchange for a committed monthly minimum. Even $1,500–$2,000/month would triple annual revenue from this one relationship and make it plannable.

### 3. PPV is a marketing problem, not a product problem
Four events per year with under 10 viewers isn't a content failure — it's an audience and marketing failure. The events exist. The infrastructure exists. The mechanism to find and convert buyers doesn't.

**The fix:** PPV cannot be treated as a standalone event. It needs an audience built around it — specifically, the BlackMarker.tv platform and social channels need to be the marketing engine that drives every PPV event.

### 4. The Build leg doesn't exist yet
Based on current clients, BMB has zero platform/website retainer clients. The highest-margin, most scalable leg of the business — custom platform builds + ongoing marketing management — has not been productized or sold. This is the biggest gap between current reality and the scaling model.

**The fix:** This needs to be the primary sales focus of Phase 1. Even 3–5 retainer clients at $1,500–$2,500/month would add $54,000–$150,000 ARR and dramatically change the financial picture.

### 5. BlackMarker.tv generates no meaningful revenue
The network exists and has programming, but it is not currently a revenue-generating asset. No subscription revenue, no sponsorships, no PPV traction.

**The fix:** This leg needs infrastructure investment and a consistent programming schedule before it can be monetized. It's a 12–18 month build — not a quick win, but essential for the long-term formula.

## What's Actually Working (Keep and Protect)

1. **Studio Enterprise relationship** — This is proof that BMB can execute recurring production work reliably. It needs to be protected, deepened, and potentially expanded (are there other universities or graduation event operators they could refer or connect?)

2. **The execution capability** — The fact that BMB delivers 2–4 professional streaming events per month means the production infrastructure works. The problem is sales and packaging, not delivery.

3. **The formula insight** — Build + Produce + Operate is the right strategy. The research confirms it. The current client base just hasn't been shaped around it yet.
