# Black Marker Media — Competitor Analysis

**Date:** 2026-04-06

## BMB Core Offering (Summary)
- Live streaming platform / website design & hosting
- Internet marketing (SEO, PPC, social media)
- Event registration systems, online stores, content management
- "One stop shop" for content creators, businesses, and event companies of any size
- Differentiator: managed/custom builds vs. self-serve SaaS

---

## Top 5 Competitors

### 1. Uscreen — [uscreen.tv](https://www.uscreen.tv)
**Type:** All-in-one SaaS video membership platform  
**Target:** Content creators, fitness instructors, online educators  
**Key Offerings:** Live streaming, branded website, mobile/TV app builder, community, subscriptions  
**Pricing:** $149/mo (Growth) — $499/mo (Pro) + per-member fees  
**Why They Compete:** Closest positioning to BMB's "one stop shop" — same audience, bundles streaming + website + monetization  
**Strength:** Strong creator-market brand, polished UX, native app builder  
**Weakness:** Self-serve SaaS (no hands-on agency service), higher cost at scale

---

### 2. Dacast — [dacast.com](https://www.dacast.com)
**Type:** Professional white-label live streaming platform  
**Target:** Businesses, broadcasters, event organizers  
**Key Offerings:** Live streaming, VOD hosting, white-label player, paywall, CDN, monetization  
**Why They Compete:** Serves same business/event audience, white-label branding competes with BMB's custom-built approach  
**Strength:** ~19% OTT market mindshare, strong feature set for pro streaming  
**Weakness:** Not a full marketing/website service — streaming infrastructure only

---

### 3. Vimeo (Livestream / OTT) — [vimeo.com](https://vimeo.com)
**Type:** Enterprise video & live streaming platform  
**Target:** Corporate event teams, marketers, media companies, creators  
**Key Offerings:** Live streaming, branded OTT site + apps, webinar tools, event registration, analytics  
**Why They Compete:** Full-stack streaming + branded site solution; ~27% OTT mindshare  
**Strength:** Premium brand recognition, enterprise features, strong analytics  
**Weakness:** Expensive at enterprise tier, less hands-on service

---

### 4. Muvi — [muvi.com](https://www.muvi.com)
**Type:** White-label OTT platform builder (managed service)  
**Target:** Businesses and media companies wanting branded streaming platforms  
**Key Offerings:** Branded streaming website + mobile/TV apps, CDN, monetization, hosting — no coding needed  
**Why They Compete:** Most similar managed-service model to BMB; clients get fully built, branded streaming infrastructure  
**Strength:** Very comprehensive platform, handles technical side end-to-end  
**Weakness:** Less marketing/SEO service; higher price point for full suite

---

### 5. Brightcove — [brightcove.com](https://www.brightcove.com)
**Type:** Enterprise video cloud platform  
**Target:** Large media companies, enterprises, corporate event organizers  
**Key Offerings:** Live streaming, VOD, marketing integrations, analytics, monetization  
**Why They Compete:** Competes for corporate event and media company clients; full video infrastructure with marketing tools  
**Strength:** Industry-leading enterprise features, global CDN, strong marketing analytics  
**Weakness:** Enterprise pricing, not suitable for small creators or small businesses

## Top 5 Live Stream Production Service Competitors

These companies compete with BMB on the **production services** side — hands-on, crew-based live streaming for events, venues, and corporate clients.

---

### 1. Beverly Boy Productions — [beverlyboy.com](https://beverlyboy.com/live-streaming-services/)
**Type:** Full-service video production & webcasting company  
**Founded:** 24+ years in business, 25,000+ projects delivered  
**Target:** Fortune 500 companies, corporate conferences, trade shows, product launches  
**Key Clients:** Disney, Viacom, Taco Bell, Comcast, Western Digital  
**Services:** Multi-camera streaming, hybrid event production, 4K HDR broadcast, interactive webcasting (polls, Q&A), multi-platform distribution (YouTube, LinkedIn, Facebook, Zoom, Teams, RTMP)  
**Pricing:** Single-camera webcast $1,500–$5,000 | Multi-camera $10,000–$25,000+  
**Reach:** Professional crews in every major U.S. city, 24/7 support  
**Strength:** Scale, brand credibility, Fortune 500 relationships, nationwide footprint  
**Weakness:** Premium pricing puts them out of reach for small/mid-size venues and creators

---

### 2. Varvid — [varvid.com](https://varvid.com/)
**Type:** Live streaming & webcasting specialist agency  
**Founded:** ~20 years in business  
**Target:** Conferences, corporate events, speaking tours, graduations, investor meetings, book signings  
**Services:** Multi-camera streaming, multi-platform simultaneous broadcast, cellular bonding for venues with poor connectivity, PPT/computer source integration, web-ready file delivery  
**Pricing:** Basic $4,999+ | Standard $7,499+ | Premium $10,999+  
**Strength:** Transparent packaged pricing, cellular bonding tech for tough venue environments, broad event-type coverage  
**Weakness:** Less brand recognition than Beverly Boy; limited geographic footprint

---

### 3. We & Goliath — [weandgoliath.com](https://weandgoliath.com/)

**Founded:** 1999  
**Target:** Global enterprises, SMBs, nonprofits, associations, conferences

### 4. AMP Event Group — [amp-events.com](https://www.amp-events.com/live-streaming-company)

## Top 5 Competitors to BlackMarker.tv (Content Network / Live Shows / PPV / Community)

**BlackMarker.tv** (Black Marker Bandit) is a live online broadcast network for independent art, entertainment, and current events. It features live talk shows, music shows, comedy, podcasts, performance competitions, PPV events, and a free streaming loop — with an audience that values independent, grassroots creators over mainstream media.

---

### 1. Twitch — [twitch.tv](https://www.twitch.tv)
**Type:** Live streaming network (Amazon-owned)  
**Founded:** 2011  
**Scale:** 240M+ monthly active users, 7.4M+ streamers/month, ~2M concurrent viewers, $1.75B estimated 2026 revenue  
**Content:** Live shows, gaming, IRL, music, podcasts, talk shows, creative content — broadcaster-driven programming  
**Monetization:** Subscriptions (streamers keep 50–70%), Bits (tipping), PPV-style channel point events  
**Interaction:** Real-time chat is central to the experience — chat drives the show, not just comments  
**Strength:** Dominant market share, massive built-in audience discovery, deep community culture  
**Weakness:** 70% male under 34 demographic skew, intense competition for visibility, strict content moderation

---

### 2. Kick — [kick.com](https://kick.com)
**Type:** Creator-first live streaming network  
**Founded:** 2022 (Australia)  
**Scale:** 4th most-watched live platform globally (Q3 2025); $46M+ paid out to creators since launch  
**Content:** Gaming, IRL, music, podcasts, talk shows — broad and loosely moderated  
**Monetization:** 95/5 subscription split (industry-best), creator incentive program, multi-streaming allowed  
**Interaction:** Live chat, community building, gifted subscriptions  
**Strength:** Best revenue share in the industry, fastest-growing platform (131% growth), very creator-friendly terms  
**Weakness:** Smaller audience than Twitch/YouTube, perception issues around gambling content roots

---

### 3. TrillerTV — [trillertv.com](https://www.trillertv.com)
**Type:** Independent live events & PPV sports/entertainment network (formerly FITE TV)  
**Scale:** 8M+ registered users, 1,000+ live events/year, 10,000+ hours of free video  
**Content:** Combat sports (boxing, MMA, BKFC), professional wrestling (AEW, GCW, NJPW), cricket, music events  
**Monetization:** PPV ($29.99–$59.99/event), subscription tiers (TrillerTV+, AEW Plus, Willow), free tier  
**Interaction:** Event-based community, live broadcast engagement  
**Strength:** Purpose-built for independent live events and PPV — closest model to what BlackMarker.tv does at scale; proven infrastructure for niche content networks  
**Weakness:** Heavily sports-focused; limited general entertainment/talk/comedy programming

---

### 4. Veeps — [veeps.com](https://www.veeps.com)
**Type:** Live music & entertainment PPV + subscription streaming network (Live Nation-owned)  
**Founded:** 2018 by Joel & Benji Madden  
**Scale:** Millions of viewers worldwide; thousands of artists; Guinness World Record holder for largest ticketed livestream by a solo male artist; Emmy-nominated; Fast Company Most Innovative Company  
**Content:** Live concerts, comedy shows, artist exclusives, on-demand replays — all artist/creator-driven  
**Monetization:** PPV per event + Veeps All Access subscription (unlimited live + on-demand)  
**Interaction:** Live chat during streams, real-time artist-to-fan direct response, emoji reactions  
**Strength:** Premium artist/fan connection model, high production values, strong entertainment niche, Live Nation distribution power  
**Weakness:** Music/entertainment-focused; less suited to talk shows, podcasts, or comedy; Live Nation backing means less "indie" feel

---

### 5. Rumble — [rumble.com](https://www.rumble.com)
**Type:** Independent video & live streaming network  
**Founded:** 2013  
**Content:** News, commentary, live shows, podcasts, talk programming, independent creators — positions itself as an alternative, free-speech-oriented network  
**Monetization:** Ad revenue sharing, Rumble Premium subscription, live streaming tipping (Rants), PPV events  
**Interaction:** Live chat, comments, community subscriptions  
**Strength:** Fastest-growing alternative streaming network; strong with independent voices, talk shows, and current events content — very aligned with BlackMarker.tv's "independent art, entertainment and current events" positioning  
**Weakness:** Content moderation controversy; demographic skews toward political commentary; less entertainment/music programming
